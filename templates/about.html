About Us

Sign Bridge is an innovative educational platform developed to support the learning needs of the deaf and hard-of-hearing community. It is powered by SiGML (Sign Gesture Markup Language) and integrates natural language processing, gesture recognition, and animated sign language avatars to deliver educational content in a fully visual and accessible format.

This project originated from an academic research initiative presented in our IEEE conference paper, where we explored how advanced technologies like HamNoSys and SiGML can be applied to generate accurate Indian Sign Language (ISL) gestures from text, audio, and video inputs.

The platform allows users to upload text, PDF documents, or audio files, which are processed and converted into ISL gestures through a multi-stage pipeline that includes speech-to-text, ISL grammar parsing, and avatar-based animation rendering. The system leverages tools like Stanford NLP and spaCy for linguistic analysis, and CWASA players for SiGML-based avatar playback.

Our goal is to bridge the communication gap and make education more inclusive by enabling real-time, visual sign language interpretations of academic material. Whether it's school textbooks, spoken lectures, or training videos, Sign Bridge ensures that every learner can access knowledge equally and effectively.
